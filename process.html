Back story

I thought long and hard about what I wanted my thesis to be centred around. When i went back to india for my winter break I constantly was thinking about what should my thesis revolve around. While at home, I could not help but retrospect how my first semester abroad was.

When i moved to US, I wanted to embrace the awkward. Be the first one to initiate conversations. Try to fully take in the experience of living in a different country and stepping out of my comfort zone.

This constant evolution was uncomfortable but rewarding. I found a community of amazing friends and colleagues in my school. I felt at home with my friends I had made and that gave me a sense of safety.

 

When i was thinking about it at home I kept seeing how much i had changed in the short amount of time I had been in school.

I constantly kept asking myself that was I loosing my true essence in order to camouflage around people and the conclusion i came to was that I did not have to hold onto my "tru self" as such.

I am constantly evolving and to thrive I need to shed my old skin to become a new.

This revelation became the core of my installation.

This project was done as a thesis for my graphic design grad program. we had ~4 months to come up with a personal topic, research, come up with design implementation to communicate the topic , build, and then install for viewers.

My classmates chose topics from all across the fields, somebody made a sculpture of their tooth to convey the story of their tooth pain, one of my classmates made pottery for a fictional design retreat and things like that.

Calculated camouflages, the title stems from the fact that we as humans constantly present doifferent versions of our selves in different environments, but that doesnt mean that we are a different person.

we are our choices and whatever we make it out to be.

"

The traditional view of authenticity as consistency

creates unnecessary pressure to remain the same, when

change is not only inevitable but often desirable.

"

The space

once i saw the space, i started scoping it out.

i thought of putting a centre attraction at the end of the room and have body tracking wall projections on both sides of the wall.

I would do this by mounting cameras to the wall at eye level and attach projectors on the opposite side in the cieling.

 

i talked to my advisor Daniel and Morgan, on what projectors and drilling resources we had on camopus to make my life easier.

I also calculated how big the projections would be and once i had my size estimations i got to designing the visuals for the centre screen.

i always wanted to do something live in the centre and not a static piece. I thought of hanging the tv on the wall and running some sort of animation to it.

but as I thought more I thoiught i could use the same tech and approach i was using for the walls on the centre piece too.

Instead of projector I would justr have the feed telecasted to the TV.

Setting it up

day 1

I was most scared about hanging the acrylic. I wanted to make the acrylic look almost floating in mid air. Apart from having no experience in any manual work, I had no idea what materials and what would be the right way to install something like this. Apart from the Acrylic I also wanted to install my TV and start seeing how the main piece would look to the audience.
